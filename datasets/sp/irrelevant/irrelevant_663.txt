 CNA The invention discloses a steering structure for a steerbywire vehicle The steering structure includes a steering knuckle a motor seat a steering bearing a steering motor and a lower transversearm the two ends of the steering knuckle extend towards one side of the steering knuckle to form a first extension portion and a second extension portion the motor seat is fixedly arranged on the first extension portion and a groove is formed in the motor seat the steering bearing is arranged in the groove an outer ring of the steering bearing and the groove are in interference fit and an inner ring of the steering bearing abuts against the first extension portion the steering bearing is a tapered roller bearing the steering motor is fixedly disposed on the motor seat and the power output end of the steering motor sequentially passes through the inner ring of the bearing and the first extension portion to be fixedly connected with the first extension portion and the lower transverse arm is fixedly arranged on one side of the motor seat By means of the structure traditional mechanical structures such as a universal joint transmission shaft are not needed and a small space is occupied The invention also provides a control method for the steering structure on the steerbywire vehicle
