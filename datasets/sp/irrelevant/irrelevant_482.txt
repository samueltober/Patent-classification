 CNU The utility model provides a service robot chassis with an independent suspension The device comprises a hub motor a chassis universal wheels a suspension spring assembly body integral supportinglugs and a walking bearing seat the two sides of the chassis are each fixedly provided with one integral supporting lug one end of the hub motor is fixed to the walking bearing seat one end of thewalking bearing seat is hinged to the corresponding integral supporting lug and the other end of the walking bearing seat is hinged to the suspension spring assembly body the universal wheels are fixed to one side of the chassis and distributed in the circumferential direction An existing chassis design often neglects the problem that driving wheels are not parallel due to machining errors and the problem of deviation in operation can be caused According to the utility model the parallelism of the driving wheel can be conveniently adjusted in the debugging stage and the operation accuracy of the chassis can be ensured on the premise of the existing machining precision
